## Facts

- OSC RPP 100 (psychology, 2015), DARPA SCORE ~3000 (social sciences), Birmingham Replication DB 1,051+; original-vs-replication pairs across multiple fields.

## Hypotheses

1. "Original vs replication" is a compound shift (sample-shift + analyst-shift + analysis-method-shift combined), not a single shift type.
2. The same method (e.g. direct replication) does not produce field-invariant success rates: some fields are categorically more replicable than others.

## Intuitions

1. 2024–25 replication meta-analyses, ML4Science / SCORE follow-ups, and an AI-prediction-of-replicability literature (predating the actual SCORE results in some cases) likely all exist.
2. An OSF preregistration-as-confounder literature probably exists.

## Unknowns

1. "Replication" is the most contested concept here — exact vs conceptual vs systematic replication produce categorically different shift mechanisms but are often pooled, with no established label separating "replication-as-discrete-shift-type."
2. The archive presupposes that an original-replication pair is the right unit; whether single-shot replications carry enough signal relative to many-lab replications cannot be determined.

## Boundary

The pair unit aligns perfectly here (study-pair = original + replication), but replications are *attempt* outcomes, so the pair carries label noise atypical of other paired datasets. **The corpus's only meta-science entry.**

## Representational commitments

- Pair-as-unit (original + replication)
- Replication types (exact / conceptual / systematic) pooled
- Success/fail/mixed outcome label
- Cross-field aggregation
