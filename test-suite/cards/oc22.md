## Facts

- 62k oxide-catalyst DFT relaxations; different exchange-correlation functional than OC20.

## Hypotheses

1. Method ordering on OC20 does not preserve under OC20→OC22 transfer: the functional change re-ranks methods.
2. "Oxide vs metal" is a phase-space shift requiring a new primitive rather than a plain compositional shift.

## Intuitions

1. The OC22 paper likely reports its own cross-OC20 baseline numbers, possibly updated by the 2024–25 literature.
2. Oxide-catalyst-specific transfer studies using experimental ground truth probably exist.

## Unknowns

1. Whether the right env unit is the *pair* (OC20-train, OC22-test) or a *joint env factor* (functional × composition) cannot be settled: the pairing tacitly assumes the former, and testing that assumption requires the pair, which is circular.

## Boundary

Same simulator caveat as OC20. Pair-only value: OC22 alone has too few samples for compositional analysis without OC20 anchoring.

## Representational commitments

- Oxide-appropriate XC functional fixed (not PBE)
- Inherits OC20's catalyst-as-element-list representation
- Single-cation oxides only
