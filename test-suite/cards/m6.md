## Facts

- 100 publicly-traded assets (50 stocks + 50 ETFs) × 12 monthly live submission rounds (Q1 2022 – Q1 2023).

## Hypotheses

1. The 2022 Fed-tightening / inflation regime did not expose method-quality differentials: returns were so noise-dominated that ranking is effectively random.
2. M6's forward-live design is a noisy in-distribution test for short-horizon near-martingale returns rather than a genuine OOD test.

## Intuitions

1. The M6 results paper (Makridakis et al. 2024) likely documents the winning methods and what they did differently.
2. Work on M5-vs-M6 transferability and finance-foundation-models probably exists post-2023.

## Unknowns

1. There is no established label for "evaluation under near-irreducible-noise" — methods compete in a regime where signal is a small fraction of variance, and whether *any* OOD measurement can resolve them within 12 rounds cannot be determined.

## Boundary

N=100 assets, 12 rounds — useful only as a forward-live design pattern, not for structural OOD study; a *methodological* anchor more than a dataset.

## Representational commitments

- 100-asset selection (50 stocks + 50 ETFs)
- Monthly round cadence
- Quintile prediction (5-class) rather than continuous return
- Live-evaluation closed after Q1 2023
